About US

Guineans Succeeding in America (GSA) is a nonprofit organization that strives to empower the Guinean-American community through education and professional development. The organization fosters and nurtures relationships between professionals and students of Guinean descent in the United States and provides resources that promote academic and professional development as well as mentorship.

GSA is an umbrella organization that consists of Guinean Graduates, a platform that highlights graduates of Guinean descent; EachOne LiftOne, a mentorship program; and the Guinean Students Association. The students association currently has chapters in four colleges, offering professional development assistance through events and workshops that foster a friendly and welcoming environment for both new and continuing students.

Baruch College

The GSA chapter of Baruch was the first GSA club/chapter to be created and which was established at Baruch over the spring of 2014 by one of the Co-founders Abdourahman Diallo, former student of Baruch College. The GSA of Baruch's main mission, as it is the main goal of the organisation as a whole, was to inspire and empower the the Guinean community through education

City College of Technology

The GSA chapter of City Tech was funded in 2015 by Thierno Diallo who is currently working at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York as a Data Scientist. The GSA of City Tech is mostly focus on helping students to get involved on campus, and train them to have the basic of leadership skills that will take them to the next level

The City College of New York

The GSA chapter of CCNY was created in 2015 and had major activities such as HTML workshops, Panel discusions, and Resume workshops.

Borough of Manhattan Community College

The GSA chapter of BMCC was recently created in the Spring 2019 and its main goal is to unify the Guinean community within all CUNY college

Fatoumata Diallo

Congratulations to Fatoumata Diallo

Fatoumata Diallo is the current president of The Guinean Students Association at Baruch College of the City University of New York. She is a rising senior at Baruch College majoring in Digital Marketing with a minor in Political Science.

This summer, Fatoumata will be interning at LinkedIn in San Francisco as part of their Client Solutions team within the LinkedIn Marketing Solutions Business. This past summer, Fatoumata had the opportunity to intern for the Financial Times as a Marketing and Communications Intern. She is also currently interning at the Walt Disney Company as a Sales Development and Marketing Intern.

Fatoumata is a big proponent of quality education for women. That is why as a freshman, Fatoumata joined AIESEC, which is the largest youth-run organization globally, where she took on over three roles, all within the course of one academic year. In addition, due to her academic excellence, Fatoumata was inducted into the Phi Eta Sigma National Honor Society. Immediately after her first year, she was nominated for an executive board position as the Marketing Director; and she served as the president of the organization for the 2017-2018 academic year.
